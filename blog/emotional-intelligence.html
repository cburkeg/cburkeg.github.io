What is Emotional Intelligence?

I would generally define emotional intelligence (or 'emotional quotient'/EQ) as a person's ability to recognise, understand, and manage emotions in themself and in other people. People with a high EQ are able to easily pick up on how others are feeling, can empathise with them and can understand their situation. They have good interpersonal skills, can communicate clearly and easily with other people, and can resolve conflicts that may arise.

Similarly, people with a high EQ are acutely aware of how they themselves are feeling and can recognise when emotions arise within themself. A person with a high EQ is able to monitor and be aware of their own emotions from a distance, rather than being lost within the emotion itself. This emotional distance helps the person manage and regulate their own emotions. People with a high EQ are more inclined towards moderation and equanimity rather than emotional outbursts or extremes.

How is EQ different to IQ?

A perosn's IQ is an abstract measure of their cognitive skill or overall intelligence (although I personally think one needs to be careful when trying to infer very much from a person's IQ - I feel these tests more a measure of how good people are at finding/matching patterns, or passing IQ tests themselves, rather than 'intelligence' in a meaningful sense.) A person's IQ supposedly represents a person's ability to solve problems, match patterns, identify relationships, and solve logical challenges. In comparison, a person's EQ is a different skillset and is independent of their IQ. A person can have a high IQ and may be able to solve complex problems, but they can simultaneously have a low EQ. For example, they may be unable to handle their own emotions (e.g. flying into rages rather than being measured and patient), incapable of empathising with others (e.g. being brusque, rude, insensitive), unable to develop friendships, etc.

Why is EQ important?

Emotional intelligence is important as it helps us understand and manage our relationship with others and with ourselves. Being emotionally intelligent lets us build self-awareness and self-control, regulate our emotions, gives us more equanimity, and more ability to deal with the vicissitudes of life. People who are emotionally intelligent are happier overall and better at stress management. Having a high emotional intelligence gives self confidence and helps with empathy and connecting with others better. This helps with communication and teamwork and improves leadership.
