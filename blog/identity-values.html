Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I recently spent some time traveling around South East Asia and wanted to visit Cambodia, particularly to see Angkor Wat. This would mean that I would have to pay the Cambodian government for a visa and for entry into Angkor Wat itself. I am certainly no expert on Cambodian politics, but I understand that Cambodia is a flawed democracy at best and is effectively ruled by a junta following a coup in the 90's, so I initially had some misgivings about this trip.

On one hand, I would be effectively enabling the current government (who go against my democratic values) by giving them money and would be fueling corruption. On the other hand, my tourism dollars could help a developing nation that is reeling from a genocide within living memory and is suffering under the long shadow of colonialism. I eventually decided that the pros of visiting the country outweighed the cons, particularly because the culture and history of the country will long outlast the contemporary government of today. However, I think these issues do need to be decided on a case by case basis, and would exclude me from visiting some countries (e.g. North Korea).

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I grew up in a pakeha househould where reading and learning was strongly encouraged. My mum was a teacher with lots of supplies for us kids, while dad had a large collection of books verging on a small library. Both mum and dad had a strong interest in reading that was passed on to me and my siblings at an early age. Both of my parents were very supportive academically and strongly valued education. They encouraged me to succeed at school and were very supportive. I think my parents are responsible for a lot of my characteristics including my interest in scholarship, reading, and learning.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

I have a tendency to be driven by negative motivators rather than positive motivators (e.g. I may be more motivated to finish an assignment by the fear of failure rather than a desire to succeed). Although this approach can be effective at achieving good outcomes, it obviously isn't the healthiest method and leads to burnout/isn't sustainable. Suffering from negative motivation can potentially restrict the full potential of possible learning offered by a course.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I previously had some friction when working on group project with other people while studying remotely for my qualification as a patent attorney. We were assigned to answer a question posed to our group, but several people within the group (including myself) had different opinions on what our answer should be. Unfortunately there were too many people placed together in our group for us to have a simple discussion about the merits of different ideas. The group also didn't have any hierarchy or a facilitator to help mediate discussion. The virtual format of the course also made it difficult to freely exchange and discuss proposed answers with one another.

Because there wasn't any way to easily discuss the pros and cons of different answers due to lack of structure within the group, I resolved my part in the conflict by not advocating for my own answer. I thought that compromising in this instance was better for group unity overall as this group assignment wasn't particularly significant. I still think this was a good decision in retrospect given the constraints of the format and the inconsequence of outcome, although it was disappointing that the solution was essentially to avoid any discussion altogether. However, if the same conflict took place in a context that was more consequential, then in the future I would make sure that my concerns were raised and discussed before we took part in shared decision-making.
