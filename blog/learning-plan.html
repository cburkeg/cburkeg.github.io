What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is a career working in tech. I would like to eventually work in a role that is fully remote or allows me to travel/live overseas, and ideally has a four-day work week. I am not sure of the exact role that I would like to take on as I know there are lots of different specialisations within tech overall, and there may be niche roles that I'm completely unaware of but would be very well suited for!

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I have a lot of experience with learning as I have studied several topics to a high level before (I have a PhD in Physics and a Masters in Intellectual Property). I therefore have many of strategies and skills to draw from that have worked in the past. I think that I'm a fast learner and am also persistent. I am happy to keep on trying to solve problem a or understand a concept. In terms of limitations, I think that I can be driven by negative motivators (e.g. fear of failure or worry about the future) rather than desire for positive outcomes, which I don't think is the healthiest way to learn or study.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I am primarily here to develop my technical skills, but I would like to develop a mindset where I am motivated more positively rather than negatively, if possible. I also think that everybody can always work on giving and receiving feedback, myself included.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

I commit that I will manage my workload by consistently and sustainably working through the material required, rather than allowing work to pile up and accummulate. I will regularly check in with my facilitator in our meetings to make sure that I am on the right track and am at the right stage. I will also make sure to seek help if I feel that I am struggling to manage the workload. In terms of working with others, I will listen to others to ensure safe and make sure they are heard and feel valued within the work environment.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

As above, I commit that I will seek help as soon as I think I could benefit from it. I will do this either in person or remotely depending on the circumstances (e.g. the nature of the help, who is available, etc).

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect the facilitation team to help me out with any technical problems when asked, in a reasonable timeframe. I also expect them to help me not only solve the problem, but to ideally understand the solution. The facilitation team should also be patient and should understand that I am here to learn.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

I have no conflicting schedules.
