Original learning plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is a career working in tech. I would like to eventually work in a role that is fully remote or allows me to travel/live overseas, and ideally has a four-day work week. I am not sure of the exact role that I would like to take on as I know there are lots of different specialisations within tech overall, and there may be niche roles that I'm completely unaware of but would be very well suited for!

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I have a lot of experience with learning as I have studied several topics to a high level before (I have a PhD in Physics and a Masters in Intellectual Property). I therefore have many of strategies and skills to draw from that have worked in the past. I think that I'm a fast learner and am also persistent. I am happy to keep on trying to solve problem a or understand a concept. In terms of limitations, I think that I can be driven by negative motivators (e.g. fear of failure or worry about the future) rather than desire for positive outcomes, which I don't think is the healthiest way to learn or study.

I am primarily here to develop my technical skills, but I would like to develop a mindset where I am motivated more positively rather than negatively, if possible. I also think that everybody can always work on giving and receiving feedback, myself included.
