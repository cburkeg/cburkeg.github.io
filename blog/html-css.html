Box model

CSS uses a 'box model' to display HTML elements on screen. Each HTML element is surrounded by a box that is drawn around the element and separates the content itself (contained within the box) from the boxes of other elements. Each box has an associated margin, border, and padding. These variables are used to buffer and separate the boxed elements from one another, and also determine the position of content with respect to its own box. Margin

The margin is the area (shown in orange above) that surrounds the box associated with the example HTML element. The size of the margin can be independently set (in absolute units or in relative units) for each side of the box. A box's margin is the distance between the border of the box and the margins of other boxes belonging to other HTML elements. In other words, if two elements are displayed side by side on a page, then the boxes surrounding those elements are separated by their combined margins. The margin is also the distance between the boxes of outer-most elements and the edge of the page. The margin does not affect the spacing of the content itself relative to the box's border.

Border

The border (shown in black in the figure above) is the outer portion of the box that surrounds the padding and content. The border separates the content from the remainder of the page and can be hidden or shown. There are many visual aspects of the border that can be changed including the border's style (e.g. solid or dashed) and its colour. The width of the border can also be varied. The border on each side of the box can be customised independently of the other sides.

Padding

The padding (shown in blue in the figure above) is the area between the content itself and the internal border of the box. The padding can be also measured in absolute or relative units and, like the margin, can be independently set for the different sides of the box. The combined size of the padding and the content itself determines the dimensions of the box and its border.

How can I learn more about an element's margin, border, and padding?

It can be difficult to determine whether the spacing of a given element is due to the element's margin or padding, as both the margin and padding are transparent. It can even be difficult to determine which margin or padding is responsible for the spacing between adjacent elements. Dev tools can be very helpful in these situations as they colour-code the margin and padding of a selected element using the above scheme. The Styles tab can also tell you where the values for the margin, border, and padding are being inherited from.
